\tag{category-theory}

\begin{dfn*}[Right Inverse; Left Inverse; Retract]
	Let $x\xrightarrow{s}y\xrightarrow{r}x$ be morphisms in a \zf{category} with
	$rs=1_x$. Then $s$ is a \emph{right inverse} to $r$, and $r$ is a \emph{left
		inverse} to $s$. $x$ is a \emph{retract} of $y$.
\end{dfn*}

Right inverses are always \zf{epimorphism}s and left inverses are always
\zf{monomorphism}s.

\begin{notation}
	We sometimes call a left inverse a \emph{section} or, to emphasize
	monomoprhism, a $\emph{split monomorphism}. We sometimes call a right inverse
	a \emph{retract} or, to emphasize epimorphism, a \emph{split epimorphism}.
\end{notation}

Right and left inverses are \zf[dual]{opposite-category} to each other.
