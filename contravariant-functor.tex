\tag{category-theory}

\begin{dfn*}[Contravariant Functor]
	A \emph{contravariant functor} $F$ between
	\zf[categories]{category} $\cat{C}$ and $\cat{D}$ is a \zf{functor} $F:
		\opc{C}\rightarrow\cat{D}$.
\end{dfn*}

Functors are generally covariant unless specified.

It is convenient to think of contravariant functors as operating directly on
$\cat{C}$, but reversing the direction of arrows. The following diagram is from
Riehl:

\begin{figure}[H]
	\centering
	\begin{tikzcd}
		\opc{C} && \cat{D} \\
		c & \mapsto & Fc \\
		& \mapsto \\
		{c'} & \mapsto & {Fc'}
		\arrow["F", from=1-1, to=1-3]
		\arrow["f"', from=2-1, to=4-1]
		\arrow["Ff"', from=4-3, to=2-3]
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{figure}
